Calvin Johnson

aka MEGATRON

"Megatron" Calvin Johnson -- one of the nicest guys to ever play the game.

Here's a time line of Calvin Johnson's life:

  • 1985 - Born in Newnan, Georgia
  • 2004 - Begins attending the Georgia Institute of Technology
  • 2004 - As a freshman, he was a first team All-Atlantic Coast Conference (ACC) selection
  • 2005 - Earns first team All-American honors, All-ACC honors for the second straight year and was semifinalist for the Fred Biletnikoff Award
  • 2006 - Wins the Biletnikoff Award, was a first-team All-ACC selection for the third consecutive year and was recognized as a unanimous first-team All-American
  • 2006 - Opts to work on a project building solar latrines to improve sanitation in Bolivia
  • 2007 - Selected by the Detroit Lions as the second pick overall in the 2007 NFL Draft, the highest a Georgia Tech player has ever been drafted. Later signs a six-year contract making him the highest paid player in Lions history (at the time)
  • 2007 - Teammate Roy Williams gives him the nickname "Megatron" due to his large hands. It quickly catches on
  • 2008 - Despite the Lions failing to win a single game all year, Johnson finishes fifth in the league in receiving yards (1,331)
  • 2010 - Earns first Pro Bowl selection of his career after amassing 77 receptions for 1,120 yards and 12 TDs during the season. Also receives the Lions/Detroit Sports Broadcasters Association/Pro Football Writers Association (Detroit Chapter) Media-Friendly Good Guy Award
  • 2011 - Finishes the season with a career-high 1,681 receiving yards and 16 touchdowns, helping the Lions clinch their first playoff berth since 1999. Although the Lions lose their playoff game, Johnson gains 211 receiving yards, breaking the team playoff record.
  • 2012 - Signs an 8-year extension worth $132 million, making him the highest-paid receiver in the league. Soon after, Johnson is selected as the Madden NFL 13 cover athlete
  • 2012 - Breaks the all-time single-season receiving yards record previously held by Jerry Rice, finishing with 1,964 yards
  • 2013 - Against the Cowboys, Johnson catches 14 of 16 passes thrown in his direction for 329 receiving yards and 1 TD. This is the highest receiving yardage ever in a regulaiton-length game and the second-highest overall single-game yardage in NFL history.
  • 2014 - Becomes the fastest player to reach 10,000 receiving yards in NFL history
  • 2015 - Breaks the Lions' franchise record for most receptions. Also selected to the Pro Bowl for the sixth consecutive year
  • 2016 - Announces he is seriously considering retirement, potentially ending the career of one of the greatest wide receivers of all-time
